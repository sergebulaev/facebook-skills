# Humanizer Examples (Facebook Page)

Worked before/after rewrites. Each shows the AI tells caught and the fix.

## Example 1 - the corporate announcement

**Before (AI-drafted):**

> We are thrilled to announce that we have officially launched our brand-new
> product, which fundamentally reimagines the way our customers leverage their
> daily workflow. This is a true game-changer and we cannot wait to embark on
> this journey with all of you. Stay tuned for more updates.

Tells caught:
- "We are thrilled to announce" (corporate auto-pilot, blocker)
- "fundamentally", "leverage", "game-changer", "embark on this journey" (AI vocab)
- No specific detail, no number, no human voice
- Way over the 80-char sweet spot with nothing earning the length

**After (rewrite, short):**

> It is live. The thing we rebuilt three times finally shipped today.

73 chars. One plain line, a real detail ("rebuilt three times"), human voice,
under the sweet spot. Designed to be shared.

## Example 2 - the buried short post

**Before:**

> In today's fast-paced world, customer service can really make or break a
> business, and we have always believed that being responsive is essentially the
> most important thing you can do. That is why our team works incredibly hard to
> get back to every single message as fast as humanly possible.

Tells caught:
- "in today's fast-paced world" (dead phrase, blocker)
- "essentially", "really" (filler)
- The actual point is one number hiding in a paragraph

**After (the short version that was hiding inside):**

> We answer every message in under 4 minutes. On weekends too.

59 chars. The number was the whole post. Everything else was padding.

## Example 3 - engagement bait

**Before:**

> Our new menu drops Friday! LIKE and SHARE this post if you are excited, and tag
> 3 friends who need to try it! Let us know in the comments below!! 🔥🔥🔥🔥

Tells caught:
- "LIKE and SHARE", "tag 3 friends" (engagement bait, downranked, blocker)
- "Let us know in the comments below" (dead closer)
- 4 emoji (cut to 0-1)

**After:**

> New menu drops Friday. Which one are you trying first?

54 chars. The question earns real comments without begging. One idea, under the
sweet spot, designed for comments.

## Example 4 - keeping a real story post (long is fine when earned)

**Before:**

> We wanted to take a moment to delve into the journey of how our bakery
> navigated a really challenging period and ultimately emerged stronger, which we
> believe is a testament to the robust resilience of our incredible team.

Tells caught:
- "delve", "navigated", "ultimately", "robust", "testament", "journey" (AI vocab)
- Abstract, no scene, no specific moment

**After (a real story post, deliberately longer, FB8 shape):**

> The oven died at 4am on our busiest Saturday.
>
> Two of us drove across town, borrowed a friend's oven, and baked 200 loaves in
> a stranger's kitchen by 9.
>
> Sold out by noon. Sometimes the backup plan is just people who show up.

A long post, but every line earns its place: a real scene, a real number, a
quotable ending built to be shared. This is when going long is the right call.
