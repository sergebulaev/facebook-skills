# Short-Post Checklist (Facebook Page)

Run every Page-post draft through this before showing the approval card.

## The under-80 sweet spot

- [ ] Did you try to land the whole point under 80 characters? That is the
      highest-engagement shape on a Page (~66% lift, reported).
- [ ] If the post is over 80 chars: is the extra length earning its place (a real
      story, a launch, a spotlight)? If not, tighten it.
- [ ] First line carries the post on its own (everything above the "See more"
      fold on mobile).

## Voice scrub

- [ ] No em dashes (`—`), en dashes (`–`), or double dashes (`--`). Use `..`.
- [ ] No AI vocab: leverage, utilize, streamline, harness, delve, foster,
      fundamentally, essentially, game-changer, deep dive.
- [ ] No "We are thrilled / excited / delighted to announce" opener.
- [ ] No rule-of-three triplet unless each item is concrete.
- [ ] Names capitalized (Stripe, Canva, Shopify).

## Substance

- [ ] One idea only. Two ideas means two posts.
- [ ] At least one specific number or named entity where the claim allows it.
- [ ] A clear primary goal: shares, comments, or reactions. Not all three.
- [ ] Designed for a share or a comment, not just a passive Like.

## Format

- [ ] 0-2 hashtags. They barely help discovery on a Page; skip unless one is a
      genuine campaign tag.
- [ ] 0-2 emoji, and only where each earns its place. None on a serious take.
- [ ] If there is a link: framing text above it, and the user knows link posts
      reach fewer people organically than a native text/photo/video post.
- [ ] Line breaks used as beats, not decoration.

## Close

- [ ] Ends on a landing or a specific question, never "What do you think?".
- [ ] No engagement bait ("LIKE and SHARE", "comment YES").

## Char-count quick reference

| Element | Value |
|---|---|
| Engagement sweet spot | under 80 chars (~66% more engagement, reported) |
| Native post limit | 63,206 chars |
| Story-post guidance | keep under ~500 chars of real substance |
| Hashtags | 0-2 |
| Emoji | 0-2 |

Lead short. The 63,206-char ceiling is a limit, not a target. Reach for the
under-80 line first, and only write long when a story, launch, or spotlight
genuinely needs the room.
